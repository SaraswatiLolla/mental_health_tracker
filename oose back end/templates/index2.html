GUIDELINES

*MILD DEPRESSION AND ANXIETY

  • Learning stress-management techniques, such as deep breathing or progressive muscle relaxation, can be helpful.
  • Practicing gratitude and positive self-talk can help shift your focus away from negative thoughts and emotions.
  • Mindfulness-based interventions, such as meditation or yoga, can help improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression.
  • Practicing good sleep hygiene, such as going to bed and waking up at the same time each day, can help regulate mood and energy levels.
  • Avoiding or reducing alcohol and drug use can help prevent depression symptoms from worsening.

*MODERATE DEPRESSION AND ANXIETY

  • Psychodynamic therapy may be recommended to help explore underlying psychological issues that contribute to depression.
  • Interpersonal therapy may be recommended to help improve communication and relationship skills.
  • Light therapy may be recommended, particularly for individuals who experience seasonal affective disorder (SAD).
  • Holistic approaches, such as herbal remedies or aromatherapy, may be recommended in addition to traditional treatments.
  • Creating a routine and setting achievable goals can help improve motivation and sense of purpose.

*SEVERE DEPRESSION AND ANXIETY

  • Intensive outpatient programs may be recommended as an alternative to inpatient treatment.
  • Alternative therapies, such as art therapy or equine therapy, may be recommended to supplement traditional treatments.
  • Combining different types of medication may be necessary to find the most effective treatment.
  • Psychoeducation, or learning more about depression and how it affects the brain and body, may be recommended to improve understanding and reduce stigma.
  • Developing a strong support network, including family, friends, and mental health professionals, can be critical to recovery.
